@Y7 HISTORY — EDWARD VI MASTER REVISION DOCUMENT

\==================================================  
EDWARD VI (1547–1553)  
\==================================================

Who was Edward VI?  
\- Edward VI was the son of Henry VIII and Jane Seymour.  
\- He became King in 1547 after Henry VIII died.  
\- Edward was too young to rule by himself.  
\- England was governed by advisers and the Council of Regency.

\--------------------------------------------------  
EDWARD VI’S ADVISERS  
\--------------------------------------------------

Edward Seymour, Duke of Somerset  
\- Edward Seymour was Edward VI’s uncle.  
\- He became Lord Protector.  
\- Somerset supported Protestant ideas.  
\- He introduced many religious changes.

John Dudley, Duke of Northumberland  
\- Northumberland later replaced Somerset.  
\- He also supported Protestantism.  
\- He became very powerful during Edward’s reign.

\==================================================  
PROTESTANT CHANGES UNDER EDWARD VI  
\==================================================

Protestant Influence  
\- Protestants wanted further reform of the Church.  
\- Thomas Cranmer helped make religious changes.

Religious Changes  
\- The Catholic Duke of Norfolk was imprisoned in the Tower.  
\- Protestants were allowed to worship more freely.  
\- The Act of Six Articles was removed.  
\- Censorship of books and sermons was relaxed.  
\- Protestants could discuss and publish their beliefs.

\--------------------------------------------------  
CHANGES TO CHURCHES  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Decorations and ornaments were removed from churches.  
\- Altars were replaced with wooden tables.  
\- Clergy dressed more simply.  
\- Religious statues were removed.

\==================================================  
ACT OF UNIFORMITY (1549)  
\==================================================

\- The Act of Uniformity made English the official language of church services.  
\- Both bread and wine were given to the people at Communion.

\==================================================  
BOOK OF COMMON PRAYER  
\==================================================

\- Archbishop Cranmer produced the Book of Common Prayer in 1549\.  
\- It introduced Protestant ideas into church services.  
\- Cranmer tried to reduce Catholic ideas about transubstantiation.

\==================================================  
THE PRAYER BOOK REBELLION (1549)  
\==================================================

Causes  
\- The rebellion began in Devon and Cornwall in April 1549\.  
\- People protested against the English Prayer Book.  
\- Rebels wanted religion returned to the way it had been under Henry VIII.  
\- They wanted heretics punished.

Events  
\- The city of Exeter was surrounded and placed under siege.  
\- Lord Russell attacked the rebels and ended the rebellion.

Consequences  
\- Somerset was criticised for the violent handling of the rebellion.  
\- Somerset was later sent to the Tower of London.  
\- Somerset was executed in 1552\.

\==================================================  
KETT’S REBELLION (1549)  
\==================================================

Causes  
\- Kett’s Rebellion began in East Anglia in July 1549\.  
\- Around 12,000 rebels protested against enclosure.  
\- Rebels were angry about rising food prices.  
\- Enclosure reduced common land available to ordinary people.

Robert Kett  
\- Robert Kett was a landowner.  
\- He believed enclosure was unfair.  
\- Kett said he was loyal to the King but opposed powerful landlords.

Rebel Demands  
\- Rebels wanted enclosure stopped.  
\- They wanted common land protected.  
\- They wanted priests prevented from buying land.

\--------------------------------------------------  
EVENTS OF KETT’S REBELLION  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Rebels occupied the city of Norwich.  
\- Edward VI and the Council offered pardons if rebels dispersed.  
\- Somerset refused to use foreign mercenaries.  
\- John Dudley, Earl of Warwick (later Duke of Northumberland), defeated the rebels.  
\- The final battle took place at Mousehold Heath.  
\- About 3000 rebels were killed.

\--------------------------------------------------  
CONSEQUENCES OF KETT’S REBELLION  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Robert Kett was charged with treason.  
\- Kett was executed in December 1549\.  
\- The rate of enclosure slowed down in Norfolk.  
\- The rebellion damaged Somerset’s reputation and helped cause his downfall.

\==================================================  
LADY JANE GREY AND THE SUCCESSION  
\==================================================

Edward VI’s Illness  
\- Edward became seriously ill by 1553\.  
\- Henry VIII’s will stated that Mary should become Queen after Edward.

Why Lady Jane Grey Was Chosen  
\- Northumberland feared Mary because she was Catholic.  
\- He wanted England to remain Protestant.  
\- Northumberland persuaded Edward to name Lady Jane Grey as heir.  
\- Lady Jane Grey married Guildford Dudley, Northumberland’s son.

\--------------------------------------------------  
LADY JANE GREY BECOMES QUEEN  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Edward VI died on 6 July 1553\.  
\- Lady Jane Grey was declared Queen on 10 July 1553\.

\--------------------------------------------------  
MARY TUDOR TAKES THE THRONE  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Mary raised support in East Anglia.  
\- Many nobles and ordinary people supported Mary.  
\- Northumberland’s forces lost support.  
\- Mary entered London after nine days.  
\- Lady Jane Grey became known as the “Nine Days Queen.”

\--------------------------------------------------  
CONSEQUENCES OF THE SUCCESSION CRISIS  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Lady Jane Grey and her husband were imprisoned in the Tower of London.  
\- Northumberland was arrested and executed.  
\- Thomas Cranmer was imprisoned in the Tower.  
\- Mary I became Queen of England.

Edward VI

\- Catholic.  
\- Became Queen after Edward VI died.

\==================================================  
KEY TERMS  
\==================================================

Protestant  
\- A Christian who supported reform of the Church and rejected some Catholic beliefs.

Reformation  
\- Religious changes that reduced the power of the Catholic Church.

Enclosure  
\- When landowners fenced off common land for private use.

Heretic  
\- Someone whose beliefs went against Church teachings.

Transubstantiation  
\- The Catholic belief that bread and wine become the body and blood of Christ.

Treason  
\- Crimes against the monarch or state.

Succession  
\- The order in which rulers inherit the throne.

Lord Protector  
\- A person ruling on behalf of a young monarch.
