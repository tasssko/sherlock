MIRACLES  
\==================================================

Definition of a Miracle  
\- A miracle is a happening that cannot be fully explained by natural events or human reason.

Blasphemy  
\- Blasphemy is showing disrespect to God.  
\- This could be misuse of God’s name or claiming to be God or equal to him.

\--------------------------------------------------  
Jesus Heals the Paralysed Man  
Mark 2: 1–12  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Jesus returned to Capernaum.  
\- Crowds gathered around him.  
\- Four men brought a paralysed man.  
\- They lowered him through the roof.  
\- Jesus first forgave the man’s sins.  
\- Teachers of the law accused Jesus of blasphemy.  
\- Jesus healed the man.  
\- The man got up and walked home.

Important Quotes  
\- “Son, your sins are forgiven.”  
\- “Who can forgive sins but God alone?”  
\- “Get up, take your mat and go home.”

Teachings and Meanings  
\- Jesus cared about spiritual health as well as physical health.  
\- Jews believed only God could forgive sins.  
\- Healing showed Jesus had God’s authority.  
\- Miracles rewarded faith.  
\- The friends showed strong faith.

\--------------------------------------------------  
Jesus Calms the Storm  
Mark 4: 35–41  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Jesus and the disciples crossed the lake.  
\- A storm began.  
\- Jesus was asleep.  
\- The disciples were afraid.  
\- Jesus calmed the storm.  
\- The wind and waves became calm.

Important Quotes  
\- “Teacher, don’t you care if we drown?”  
\- “Quiet\! Be still\!”  
\- “Who is this? Even the wind and waves obey him\!”

Teachings and Meanings  
\- People believed storms were caused by evil spirits.  
\- Jesus showed authority over nature.  
\- The story shows Jesus’ human side because he slept.  
\- The story shows Jesus’ divine power because he calmed the storm.  
\- The storm symbolises worries and difficulties in life.  
\- The disciples still did not fully understand Jesus.

\--------------------------------------------------  
The Feeding of the Five Thousand  
Mark 6: 30–44  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Crowds followed Jesus.  
\- The disciples worried about feeding everyone.  
\- Jesus used five loaves and two fish.  
\- Everyone ate.  
\- Twelve baskets of leftovers remained.

Important Quotes  
\- “You give them something to eat.”  
\- “Five – and two fish.”

Teachings and Meanings  
\- The disciples wanted to send the crowd away.  
\- Jesus wanted to help.  
\- Some believe Jesus created food miraculously.  
\- Others believe Jesus inspired people to share.  
\- Jesus cared for people’s needs.

\--------------------------------------------------  
Miracles Today  
\--------------------------------------------------

Beliefs  
\- Some people believe God still performs miracles today.  
\- Others believe miracles are things not yet understood.  
\- Some believe miracles do not have to come from God.

PARABLES  
\==================================================

Definition of a Parable  
\- Jesus used stories related to everyday life to explain God’s message.

\--------------------------------------------------  
The Good Samaritan  
Luke 10: 25–37  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- A lawyer asked Jesus how to inherit eternal life.  
\- Jesus taught that people should love God and love their neighbour.  
\- A man travelling from Jerusalem to Jericho was attacked.  
\- A priest ignored him.  
\- A Levite ignored him.  
\- A Samaritan helped him.  
\- The Samaritan bandaged wounds and paid for care.

Important Quotes  
\- “Love your neighbour as yourself.”  
\- “Who is my neighbour?”  
\- “Go and do likewise.”

Teachings and Meanings  
\- Everyone is your neighbour.  
\- Samaritans were hated by Jews.  
\- The Samaritan showed compassion.  
\- Love and kindness matter more than status or background.  
\- People should overcome prejudice.

\--------------------------------------------------  
The Lost Son  
Luke 15: 11–32  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- A younger son asked for his inheritance.  
\- He wasted his money.  
\- He became poor and hungry.  
\- He returned home.  
\- His father welcomed him back.  
\- The older brother became jealous.

Important Quotes  
\- “Father, I have sinned.”  
\- “This son of mine was dead and is alive again.”  
\- “He was lost and is found.”

Teachings and Meanings  
\- God forgives people who are truly sorry.  
\- Heaven rejoices when sinners repent.  
\- The father represents forgiveness.  
\- The older brother represents the Pharisees.  
\- People should support others trying to improve.

THE CRUCIFIXION  
\==================================================

The Sentence, Crucifixion and Burial  
Mark Chapter 15

\--------------------------------------------------  
Jesus Before Pilate  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Jewish leaders wanted Jesus dead.  
\- Jesus was accused of claiming to be King of the Jews.  
\- Pilate questioned Jesus.  
\- Barabbas was released instead.  
\- Jesus was flogged and condemned.

Important Quotes  
\- “Are you the king of the Jews?”  
\- “Crucify him\!”

Teachings and Meanings  
\- Jewish leaders believed Jesus threatened their authority.  
\- Jesus was accused of blasphemy.  
\- The Sanhedrin could not execute Jesus themselves.  
\- Under Roman law Jesus could be punished for claiming kingship.

\--------------------------------------------------  
The Soldiers Mock Jesus  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Soldiers dressed Jesus in purple.  
\- They placed a crown of thorns on him.  
\- They mocked and beat him.

Important Quotes  
\- “Hail, king of the Jews\!”

\--------------------------------------------------  
The Crucifixion of Jesus  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Jesus carried the cross.  
\- Jesus was crucified at Golgotha.  
\- Two rebels were crucified beside him.  
\- Crowds mocked Jesus.  
\- Darkness covered the land.  
\- Jesus died.  
\- The temple curtain tore in two.

Important Quotes  
\- “My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?”  
\- “Surely this man was the Son of God\!”

Teachings and Meanings  
\- Jesus resisted temptation to save himself.  
\- Christians believe Jesus sacrificed himself for human sin.  
\- Jesus’ death removed the barrier between humans and God.  
\- Jesus’ sacrifice was once and for all.  
\- Atonement means restoring a good relationship with God.

\--------------------------------------------------  
The Burial of Jesus  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Joseph of Arimathea buried Jesus.  
\- Jesus was placed in a tomb.  
\- A stone covered the entrance.

Important Quotes

\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Jesus appeared in a locked room.  
\- Jesus said “Peace be with you.”

\--------------------------------------------------  
Jesus Appears to Thomas  
\--------------------------------------------------

Key Events  
\- Thomas doubted the resurrection.  
\- Jesus appeared again.  
\- Jesus showed Thomas his wounds.  
\- Thomas believed.

\- “Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed.”

\==================================================  
KEY RELIGIOUS TERMS  
\==================================================

Miracle  
\- A happening that cannot be fully explained by natural events or human reason.

Blasphemy  
\- Showing disrespect to God or claiming equality with God.

Atonement  
\- Restoring a good relationship with God through sacrifice.

Faith  
\- Belief and trust without physical proof.

Resurrection  
\- Rising from the dead.

Parable  
\- A story used to explain God’s message.
