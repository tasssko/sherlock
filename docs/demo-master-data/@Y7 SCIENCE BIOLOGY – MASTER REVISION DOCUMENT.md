TOPIC A — CELLS & MICROSCOPES  
\==================================================

CELLS  
\- Cells are the basic building blocks of living organisms.  
\- Animals and plants are made of many cells.  
\- Different cells have different functions.

ANIMAL CELLS  
Animal cells contain:  
\- nucleus  
\- cytoplasm  
\- cell membrane

PLANT CELLS  
Plant cells contain:  
\- nucleus  
\- cytoplasm  
\- cell membrane  
\- cell wall  
\- chloroplasts  
\- permanent vacuole

FUNCTIONS OF CELL PARTS  
\- Nucleus controls the activities of the cell.  
\- Cytoplasm is where chemical reactions happen.  
\- Cell membrane controls what enters and leaves the cell.  
\- Cell wall strengthens and supports the plant cell.  
\- Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll for photosynthesis.  
\- Vacuole contains cell sap.

MICROSCOPES  
\- Microscopes are used to magnify tiny objects.  
\- Cells can be viewed using a microscope.

TOPIC B — BREATHING & RESPIRATION  
\==================================================

RESPIRATION  
\- Respiration is a chemical process that releases energy from glucose.  
\- Respiration happens in living cells.

AEROBIC RESPIRATION  
\- Aerobic respiration uses oxygen.  
\- Aerobic respiration releases a large amount of energy.

WORD EQUATION:  
glucose \+ oxygen → carbon dioxide \+ water \+ energy

ANAEROBIC RESPIRATION  
\- Anaerobic respiration happens without oxygen.  
\- Anaerobic respiration is less efficient than aerobic respiration.  
\- Less energy is released.  
\- Anaerobic respiration happens when cells cannot get enough oxygen.

ANAEROBIC RESPIRATION IN HUMANS  
\- In animals, anaerobic respiration produces lactic acid.  
\- Lactic acid can build up in muscles during exercise.

WORD EQUATION:  
glucose → lactic acid \+ some energy

ANAEROBIC RESPIRATION IN PLANTS AND YEAST  
\- In plants and yeast, anaerobic respiration produces carbon dioxide and alcohol.  
\- The alcohol produced is ethanol.

WORD EQUATION:  
glucose → carbon dioxide \+ alcohol \+ some energy

COMPARING AEROBIC AND ANAEROBIC RESPIRATION  
Aerobic respiration:  
\- uses oxygen  
\- produces carbon dioxide and water  
\- releases a large amount of energy

Anaerobic respiration:  
\- does not use oxygen  
\- produces lactic acid OR carbon dioxide and alcohol  
\- releases a small amount of energy

FERMENTATION  
\- Fermentation happens when yeast respires anaerobically.  
\- Yeast uses glucose during fermentation.  
\- Fermentation produces carbon dioxide and alcohol.

USES OF FERMENTATION  
Bread making:  
\- Yeast is mixed with flour, water and sugar.  
\- Carbon dioxide gets trapped in bubbles and makes the dough rise.  
\- Alcohol evaporates during baking.

Beer and wine making:  
\- Yeast ferments glucose.  
\- Carbon dioxide can make drinks fizzy.  
\- Alcohol is produced.

INVESTIGATING FERMENTATION  
\- Sugar, yeast and water are mixed together.  
\- Oil can be added to stop oxygen entering.  
\- Carbon dioxide bubbles can be counted.  
\- More bubbles means faster fermentation.  
\- Temperature affects the rate of fermentation.

TOPIC C — CLASSIFICATION  
\==================================================

CLASSIFICATION  
\- Living organisms can be classified into groups.  
\- Classification helps scientists organise living things.  
\- Organisms with similar features are grouped together.

VERTEBRATES  
Vertebrates have backbones.

The five vertebrate groups are:  
\- mammals  
\- birds  
\- fish  
\- reptiles  
\- amphibians

MAMMALS  
\- have hair or fur  
\- produce milk for young  
\- warm-blooded

BIRDS  
\- have feathers  
\- lay eggs  
\- warm-blooded

FISH  
\- have gills  
\- live in water  
\- cold-blooded

REPTILES  
\- dry scaly skin  
\- lay eggs  
\- cold-blooded

AMPHIBIANS  
\- live on land and in water  
\- moist skin  
\- cold-blooded

INVERTEBRATES  
\- Invertebrates do not have backbones.

TOPIC D — NUTRITION  
\==================================================

NUTRIENTS  
The body needs:  
\- carbohydrates  
\- proteins  
\- fats  
\- vitamins  
\- minerals  
\- fibre  
\- water

CARBOHYDRATES  
\- Carbohydrates provide energy.

PROTEINS  
\- Proteins are needed for growth and repair.

FATS  
\- Fats store energy.  
\- Fats help keep the body warm.

FIBRE  
\- Fibre helps food move through the digestive system.

WATER  
\- Water is needed for chemical reactions and transport.

BALANCED DIET  
\- A balanced diet contains the correct amounts of nutrients.

FOOD TESTS

BENEDICT’S TEST FOR SUGAR  
Method:  
\- Add Benedict’s solution to the food sample.  
\- Heat in hot water.

Result:  
\- Blue \= no sugar  
\- Green/yellow/orange/brick-red \= sugar present

IODINE TEST FOR STARCH  
Method:  
\- Add iodine solution to the food sample.

Result:  
\- Brown/orange \= no starch  
\- Blue-black \= starch present

BIURET TEST FOR PROTEIN  
Method:  
\- Add Biuret solution to the food sample.

Result:  
\- Blue \= no protein  
\- Purple/lilac \= protein present
