\==================================================  
TOPIC A — PARTICLES AND STATES OF MATTER  
\==================================================

THE PARTICLE MODEL  
\- All substances are made of tiny particles.  
\- Particles are always moving.  
\- Heating particles makes them move faster.  
\- Cooling particles makes them move slower.

\==================================================  
SOLIDS  
\==================================================

Properties of Solids  
\- Particles are packed closely together.  
\- Particles vibrate in fixed positions.  
\- Solids keep their shape.  
\- Solids keep the same volume.  
\- Solids are difficult to compress.

\==================================================  
LIQUIDS  
\==================================================

Properties of Liquids  
\- Particles are close together but can move past each other.  
\- Liquids flow.  
\- Liquids change shape to fit their container.  
\- Liquids keep the same volume.

\==================================================  
GASES  
\==================================================

Properties of Gases  
\- Particles are far apart.  
\- Particles move quickly in all directions.  
\- Gases spread out to fill a container.  
\- Gases can be compressed easily.

\==================================================  
CHANGES OF STATE  
\==================================================

Melting  
\- solid → liquid

Freezing  
\- liquid → solid

Boiling  
\- liquid → gas

Condensing  
\- gas → liquid

Evaporation  
\- liquid particles escape from the surface.

\==================================================  
DIFFUSION  
\==================================================

Diffusion  
\- Diffusion is the spreading out of particles.

Diffusion in Gases  
\- Gas particles diffuse quickly.

Diffusion in Liquids  
\- Liquid particles diffuse more slowly.

Temperature and Diffusion  
\- Higher temperatures increase particle movement.  
\- Diffusion happens faster at higher temperatures.

TOPIC B — ACIDS & ALKALIS  
\==================================================

Acids  
\- Acids have a pH below 7\.

Examples of Acids  
\- hydrochloric acid  
\- sulfuric acid

Alkalis  
\- Alkalis are soluble bases.  
\- Alkalis have a pH above 7\.

Examples of Alkalis  
\- sodium hydroxide  
\- ammonia solution

Neutral  
\- Neutral substances have a pH of 7\.

\==================================================  
THE pH SCALE  
\==================================================

pH Scale  
\- 0–6 \= acidic  
\- 7 \= neutral  
\- 8–14 \= alkaline

Strong Acids  
\- Lower pH values.

Weak Acids  
\- Higher acidic pH values.

\==================================================  
INDICATORS  
\==================================================

Universal Indicator  
\- Universal indicator changes colour depending on pH.

Indicator Colours  
\- red/orange/yellow \= acidic  
\- green \= neutral  
\- blue/purple \= alkaline

\==================================================  
NEUTRALISATION  
\==================================================

Neutralisation  
\- Acids and alkalis react together in neutralisation reactions.  
\- Neutralisation produces a neutral solution.

General Equation  
\- acid \+ alkali → salt \+ water

\==================================================  
OXIDATION  
\==================================================

Oxidation  
\- Oxidation involves oxygen.

Examples  
\- burning  
\- rusting

Rusting  
\- Iron reacts with oxygen and water.

Preventing Rust  
\- painting  
\- oiling  
\- galvanising

TOPIC D — SEPARATING MIXTURES  
\==================================================

Separating Mixtures  
\- Mixtures can be separated using physical methods.

\==================================================  
FILTRATION  
\==================================================

Filtration  
\- Filtration separates an insoluble solid from a liquid.

Equipment  
\- filter funnel  
\- filter paper  
\- beaker  
\- conical flask

Residue  
\- The solid left in the filter paper.

Filtrate  
\- The liquid collected after filtration.

\==================================================  
EVAPORATION  
\==================================================

Evaporation  
\- Evaporation separates a dissolved solid from a liquid.

Method  
1\. Pour solution into an evaporating dish.  
2\. Heat gently.  
3\. Liquid evaporates.  
4\. Solid remains.

\==================================================  
DISTILLATION  
\==================================================

Simple Distillation  
\- Distillation separates a solvent from a solution.

Method  
1\. Heat the mixture.  
2\. The liquid evaporates.  
3\. Vapour enters the condenser.  
4\. The condenser cools the vapour.  
5\. Vapour condenses back into liquid.  
6\. Pure liquid is collected.

Condenser  
\- Changes vapour into liquid.

Uses of Distillation  
\- Separating water from seawater.  
\- Producing pure water.

\==================================================  
CHROMATOGRAPHY  
\==================================================

Chromatography  
\- Chromatography separates dyes in inks and food colourings.

Method  
1\. Draw a pencil baseline.  
2\. Place ink spots on the line.  
3\. Put paper into solvent.  
4\. Solvent moves up the paper.  
5\. Dyes separate into spots.

Important Points  
\- Pencil is used because it does not dissolve.  
\- Solvent must stay below the baseline.

Chromatograms  
\- The pattern of spots is called a chromatogram.  
\- Pure substances produce one spot.  
\- Mixtures produce more than one spot.

Solubility and Chromatography  
\- More soluble dyes travel further.  
\- Different solvents give different results.

\==================================================  
WATER AND SOLUTIONS  
\==================================================

Water as a Solvent  
\- Water is a useful solvent.  
\- Many substances dissolve in water.

Seawater  
\- Seawater contains dissolved salts.

Tap Water  
\- Tap water contains dissolved substances.

Distilled Water  
\- Distilled water is pure water.  
\- Distilled water contains no dissolved substances.

\==================================================  
ICE AND WATER  
\==================================================

Water Freezing  
\- Most substances become denser when frozen.  
\- Water is unusual.

Ice  
\- Ice is less dense than liquid water.  
\- Water expands when frozen.  
\- Ice floats on water.
