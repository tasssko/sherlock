@Y7 GEOGRAPHY COASTS – MASTER REVISION DOCUMENT

\==================================================  
WHAT IS A COAST?  
\==================================================

\- The coastline is where the land meets the sea.  
\- Coasts are always changing because of:  
  \- Erosion \= breaking down rock  
  \- Transportation \= moving material  
  \- Deposition \= dropping material  
\- Over 3 million people in the UK live near the coast.

\--------------------------------------------------  
Benefits of the Coast  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Jobs (e.g. tourism, fishing)  
\- Ports for trade and transport  
\- Holiday and leisure activities

\--------------------------------------------------  
Problems at the Coast  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Flooding  
\- Cliff collapse  
\- Damage to homes and land

\==================================================  
EROSION  
(Breaks down AND moves rock)  
\==================================================

You must know the four types of erosion:

\--------------------------------------------------  
1\. Hydraulic Action  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Waves force air and water into cracks  
\- Pressure makes cracks bigger  
\- Rock breaks off

\--------------------------------------------------  
2\. Abrasion  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Rocks in waves scrape the cliff  
\- Like sandpaper

\--------------------------------------------------  
3\. Attrition  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Rocks in the sea crash together  
\- Rocks become smoother and smaller

\--------------------------------------------------  
4\. Corrosion (Solution)  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Rocks like limestone dissolve in seawater

\==================================================  
WEATHERING  
(Breaks rocks down – NO movement)  
\==================================================

\--------------------------------------------------  
1\. Freeze-Thaw Weathering  
\--------------------------------------------------

1\. Water enters cracks  
2\. Water freezes and expands  
3\. Crack widens  
4\. Ice melts and water goes deeper  
5\. Repeated action splits the rock

\--------------------------------------------------  
2\. Biological Weathering  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Plant roots grow into cracks  
\- Roots force rocks apart

\--------------------------------------------------  
3\. Chemical Weathering  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Acid rain or seawater dissolves certain rocks  
\- Examples: limestone and chalk

\==================================================  
TRANSPORTATION  
(Movement of material)  
\==================================================

Four ways the sea moves material:

\--------------------------------------------------  
1\. Traction  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Big rocks roll along the seabed

\--------------------------------------------------  
2\. Saltation  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Pebbles bounce along

\--------------------------------------------------  
3\. Suspension  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Sand floats in the water

\--------------------------------------------------  
4\. Solution  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Minerals dissolve in the water

\==================================================  
LONGSHORE DRIFT  
\==================================================

1\. Waves hit the beach at an angle  
2\. Swash moves material up the beach at an angle  
3\. Backwash moves straight down  
4\. Material moves in a zig-zag pattern along the coast

\==================================================  
DEPOSITION  
(Dropping material)  
\==================================================

\- Happens when waves lose energy

Creates:  
\- Beaches  
\- Spits  
\- Bars

\==================================================  
COASTAL LANDFORMS FROM EROSION  
\==================================================

\--------------------------------------------------  
Headlands and Bays  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Form on coastlines with hard and soft rock  
\- Soft rock erodes quickly forming bays  
\- Hard rock erodes slowly forming headlands

\--------------------------------------------------  
Cliffs and Wave-Cut Platforms  
\--------------------------------------------------

1\. Waves erode the cliff base  
2\. A wave-cut notch forms  
3\. Cliff collapses  
4\. Material is washed away  
5\. A wave-cut platform is left  
6\. Process repeats as cliff retreats

\--------------------------------------------------  
Caves, Arches, Stacks and Stumps  
\--------------------------------------------------

1\. Crack forms  
2\. Crack becomes a cave  
3\. Cave becomes an arch  
4\. Arch collapses forming a stack  
5\. Stack erodes forming a stump

\==================================================  
COASTAL DEFENCES  
\==================================================

\--------------------------------------------------  
1\. Hard Engineering  
(Expensive but effective)  
\--------------------------------------------------

Sea Walls  
\- Reflect wave energy  
\- Long-lasting  
\- Very expensive

Groynes  
\- Trap sand  
\- Build beaches  
\- Can cause erosion further along the coast

Rock Armour (Riprap)  
\- Big boulders absorb wave energy  
\- Cheaper than sea walls

Gabions  
\- Wire cages filled with rocks  
\- Absorb wave energy

\--------------------------------------------------  
2\. Soft Engineering  
(Cheaper and more natural)  
\--------------------------------------------------

Beach Nourishment  
\- Adds more sand  
\- Protects cliffs  
\- Needs repeating

Dune Regeneration  
\- Fences and plants protect dunes  
\- Natural barrier  
\- Easily damaged

Managed Retreat  
\- Allows areas to flood  
\- Creates salt marshes  
\- Some land is lost

\==================================================  
COASTAL MANAGEMENT  
\==================================================

Local councils can:  
\- Hold the line \= protect the coastline  
\- Do nothing \= let nature take its course

Decisions depend on:  
\- Cost  
\- Risk  
\- Value of the land
