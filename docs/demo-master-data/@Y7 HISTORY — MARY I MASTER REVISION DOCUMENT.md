@Y7 HISTORY — MARY I MASTER REVISION DOCUMENT

\==================================================  
MARY I (1553–1558)  
\==================================================

Who was Mary I?  
\- Mary I was the daughter of Henry VIII and Catherine of Aragon.  
\- She became Queen of England in 1553 after Edward VI died.  
\- Mary was a Catholic.  
\- She wanted England to return to Roman Catholicism.

\==================================================  
MARY I BECOMES QUEEN  
\==================================================

\- Edward VI became seriously ill in 1553\.  
\- Henry VIII’s will stated that Mary should become Queen after Edward.  
\- Northumberland feared Mary because she was Catholic.  
\- Northumberland persuaded Edward VI to name Lady Jane Grey as heir instead.

\--------------------------------------------------  
LADY JANE GREY  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Lady Jane Grey was declared Queen on 10 July 1553\.  
\- She was supported by Northumberland.  
\- Mary gathered support in East Anglia.  
\- Many nobles and ordinary people supported Mary.  
\- Northumberland’s support collapsed.  
\- Mary entered London after nine days.  
\- Lady Jane Grey became known as the “Nine Days Queen.”

\--------------------------------------------------  
CONSEQUENCES  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Lady Jane Grey and her husband were imprisoned in the Tower of London.  
\- Northumberland was arrested and executed.  
\- Thomas Cranmer was imprisoned in the Tower.  
\- Mary I officially became Queen of England.

\==================================================  
MARY I AND RELIGION  
\==================================================

\- Mary I was a committed Catholic.  
\- She wanted to reverse Protestant reforms introduced under Edward VI.  
\- Mary attempted to restore Roman Catholicism in England.

\--------------------------------------------------  
RELIGIOUS CHANGES  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Catholic worship was restored.  
\- Protestant reforms from Edward VI’s reign were reversed.  
\- England returned to the authority of the Pope.

\==================================================  
MARY I AND PROTESTANTS  
\==================================================

\- Mary believed Protestantism was dangerous and wrong.  
\- Protestants who refused to return to Catholicism were punished.

\--------------------------------------------------  
THE MARTYRS  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Many Protestants were burned at the stake during Mary’s reign.  
\- These executions earned Mary the nickname “Bloody Mary.”  
\- Thomas Cranmer was executed during Mary’s reign.  
\- Protestant martyrs became important symbols for later Protestants.

\==================================================  
MARY I AND PHILIP II OF SPAIN  
\==================================================

\- Mary married Philip II of Spain.  
\- Philip was a Catholic prince and later King of Spain.  
\- Many English people disliked the marriage.  
\- People feared England would become controlled by Spain.

\==================================================  
WYATT’S REBELLION (1554)  
\==================================================

Causes  
\- Many people opposed Mary’s planned marriage to Philip of Spain.  
\- Protestants feared the return of Catholicism.  
\- Some nobles wanted Elizabeth placed on the throne instead.

Events  
\- Sir Thomas Wyatt led the rebellion.  
\- Rebels marched towards London.  
\- Mary gave a speech encouraging loyalty.  
\- The rebellion failed.

Consequences  
\- Wyatt was executed.  
\- Lady Jane Grey was executed after the rebellion.  
\- Elizabeth was questioned and temporarily imprisoned in the Tower of London.

\==================================================  
MARY I’S PROBLEMS  
\==================================================

Religious Problems  
\- England remained divided between Catholics and Protestants.  
\- Many people disliked religious persecution.

Marriage Problems  
\- Mary’s marriage to Philip of Spain was unpopular.  
\- Philip spent little time in England.

Foreign Policy Problems  
\- England became involved in war with France because of Spain.  
\- England lost Calais in 1558\.  
\- Calais had been England’s last possession in France.

Succession Problems  
\- Mary failed to produce an heir.  
\- This increased fears about the future succession.

\==================================================  
MARY I’S LEGACY  
\==================================================

\- Mary restored Catholicism temporarily.  
\- Protestantism returned after her death under Elizabeth I.  
\- Mary’s executions damaged her reputation.  
\- Her reign is often remembered for religious persecution.

\==================================================  
IMPORTANT PEOPLE  
\==================================================

Mary I  
\- Queen of England from 1553–1558.  
\- Catholic daughter of Henry VIII and Catherine of Aragon.

Philip II of Spain  
\- Mary’s husband.  
\- Catholic ruler of Spain.

Lady Jane Grey  
\- Declared Queen before Mary took the throne.  
\- Known as the “Nine Days Queen.”

Thomas Cranmer  
\- Protestant Archbishop of Canterbury.  
\- Burned during Mary’s reign.

Sir Thomas Wyatt  
\- Leader of Wyatt’s Rebellion.

Elizabeth  
\- Mary’s half-sister.  
\- Future Elizabeth I.

Northumberland  
\- Adviser to Edward VI.  
\- Supported Lady Jane Grey against Mary.

\==================================================  
KEY TERMS  
\==================================================

Catholic  
\- A member of the Roman Catholic Church.

Protestant  
\- A Christian supporting reform of the Church.

Martyr  
\- A person killed because of their religious beliefs.

Heresy  
\- Beliefs that go against official Church teaching.

Stake  
\- Wooden post used during executions by burning.

Succession  
\- The order in which rulers inherit the throne.

Treason  
\- Crimes against the monarch or country.
