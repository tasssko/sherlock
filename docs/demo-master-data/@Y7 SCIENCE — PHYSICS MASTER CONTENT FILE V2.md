\==================================================  
TOPIC A — ENERGY  
\==================================================

ENERGY  
\- Energy can be transferred from one store to another.  
\- Energy cannot be created or destroyed.  
\- This is called the Law of Conservation of Energy.

LAW OF CONSERVATION OF ENERGY  
\- Energy can never be created or destroyed.  
\- Energy is only transferred between stores.

ENERGY TRANSFERS  
\- Useful devices transfer energy from one store to another.  
\- Some energy is always wasted.  
\- Wasted energy is usually transferred as heat.

EXAMPLES OF ENERGY TRANSFERS  
Power stations:  
\- Fossil fuels are burned to generate electricity.  
\- Some energy is wasted as heat.

Car engines:  
\- Chemical energy in fuel is transferred to kinetic energy.  
\- Some energy is wasted as heat and sound.

Light Bulbs  
\- Electrical energy is transferred into:  
  \- light energy (useful)  
  \- heat energy (wasted)

\==================================================  
RENEWABLE AND NON-RENEWABLE ENERGY  
\==================================================

NON-RENEWABLE ENERGY RESOURCES  
Examples:  
\- coal  
\- oil  
\- natural gas  
\- nuclear fuel

Advantages  
\- Produce large amounts of energy.  
\- Reliable energy supply.  
\- Do not depend on weather.

Disadvantages  
\- Limited supply.  
\- Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide.  
\- Carbon dioxide contributes to climate change.  
\- Burning fuels can release sulfur dioxide causing acid rain.  
\- Nuclear fuels produce radioactive waste.

\==================================================  
RENEWABLE ENERGY RESOURCES  
\==================================================

Examples:  
\- wind  
\- solar  
\- hydroelectric  
\- wave  
\- tidal  
\- geothermal  
\- biomass

Advantages  
\- Will not run out.  
\- Produce little pollution once operating.  
\- Fuel is free.

Disadvantages  
\- Can depend on weather.  
\- Often produce less energy.  
\- Expensive to build.  
\- Can affect local environments.

\==================================================  
GENERATING ELECTRICITY  
\==================================================

Wind Power  
\- Wind turns turbine blades.  
\- The turbine drives a generator.

Hydroelectric Power  
\- Water stored behind a dam flows through turbines.  
\- Turbines drive generators.

Wave Power  
\- Waves move turbines.  
\- Turbines drive generators.

Solar Power  
\- Solar cells convert sunlight into electricity.

Geothermal Power  
\- Water is heated underground.  
\- Steam turns turbines.

Tidal Power  
\- Tides move water through turbines.

Biomass  
\- Biomass stores the Sun’s energy.  
\- Biomass can be burned to generate electricity.

\==================================================  
TOPIC B — FORCES  
\==================================================

FORCES  
\- Forces are pushes or pulls.  
\- Forces can change speed, direction or shape.

Effects of Forces  
A force can:  
\- start movement  
\- stop movement  
\- speed up an object  
\- slow down an object  
\- change direction  
\- change shape

Forces are measured in:  
\- newtons (N)

\==================================================  
BALANCED AND UNBALANCED FORCES  
\==================================================

Balanced Forces  
\- Balanced forces are equal in size and opposite in direction.  
\- Balanced forces do not change movement.  
\- An object may:  
  \- remain still  
  \- move at constant speed

Unbalanced Forces  
\- Unbalanced forces cause changes in movement.  
\- Objects may:  
  \- speed up  
  \- slow down  
  \- change direction

\==================================================  
FRICTION  
\==================================================

Friction  
\- Friction is a force that opposes movement between surfaces.

Advantages of Friction  
\- Helps objects stop.  
\- Provides grip.  
\- Helps people walk safely.

Disadvantages of Friction  
\- Wastes energy as heat.  
\- Causes wear.  
\- Slows objects down.

\==================================================  
AIR RESISTANCE  
\==================================================

Air Resistance  
\- Air resistance is a frictional force.  
\- Air resistance acts against moving objects in air.

Parachutes  
\- Larger parachutes create greater air resistance.  
\- Greater air resistance slows falling objects.

Skydivers  
\- Gravity pulls downward.  
\- Air resistance pushes upward.  
\- Eventually forces become balanced.  
\- The skydiver then falls at constant speed.

\==================================================  
WATER RESISTANCE  
\==================================================

Water Resistance  
\- Water resistance acts against objects moving through water.  
\- Streamlined shapes reduce water resistance.

\==================================================  
GRAVITY  
\==================================================

Gravity  
\- Gravity is a force that attracts masses.  
\- Earth’s gravity pulls objects toward the centre of Earth.

Mass  
\- Mass is the amount of matter in an object.  
\- Mass does not change location to location.  
\- Mass is measured in kilograms (kg).

Weight  
\- Weight is the force caused by gravity.  
\- Weight changes depending on gravitational field strength.  
\- Weight is measured in newtons (N).

Weight Formula  
\- Weight \= mass × gravitational field strength  
\- W \= m × g

Where:  
\- W \= weight (N)  
\- m \= mass (kg)  
\- g \= gravitational field strength (N/kg)

Values of g  
\- Earth \= 10 N/kg  
\- Mars \= 3.7 N/kg

\==================================================  
SPEED  
\==================================================

Speed  
\- Speed measures how fast something moves.

Speed Formula  
\- Speed \= distance ÷ time  
\- s \= d ÷ t

Distance Formula  
\- Distance \= speed × time  
\- d \= s × t

Time Formula  
\- Time \= distance ÷ speed  
\- t \= d ÷ s

Units of Speed  
\- metres per second (m/s)  
\- kilometres per hour (km/h)  
\- miles per hour (mph)

Time Conversions  
\- 1 hour \= 3600 seconds

\==================================================  
SPRINGS  
\==================================================

Deformation  
\- Forces can stretch or compress objects.

Elastic Objects  
\- Elastic objects return to their original shape.

Extension  
\- Extension \= stretched length − original length

Compression  
\- Compression \= original length − compressed length

Spring Investigations  
\- Greater force causes greater extension.  
\- Different masses stretch springs by different amounts.

TOPIC C — DENSITY  
\==================================================

DENSITY  
\- Density describes how much mass is in a given volume.

Density Formula  
\- Density \= mass ÷ volume  
\- D \= m ÷ V

Mass Formula  
\- Mass \= density × volume  
\- m \= D × V

Volume Formula  
\- Volume \= mass ÷ density  
\- V \= m ÷ D

Units of Density  
\- g/cm³  
\- kg/m³

\==================================================  
MEASURING DENSITY  
\==================================================

Regular Objects  
1\. Measure mass with a balance.  
2\. Measure dimensions.  
3\. Calculate volume.  
4\. Calculate density.

Volume of Regular Objects  
\- Volume \= length × width × height

Irregular Objects  
1\. Put water into a measuring cylinder.  
2\. Record the starting volume.  
3\. Submerge the object fully.  
4\. Record the final volume.  
5\. Final volume − starting volume \= object volume.

Liquids  
1\. Measure volume.  
2\. Measure mass.  
3\. Calculate density.

\==================================================  
FLOATING AND SINKING  
\==================================================

Floating  
\- Less dense substances float on more dense substances.

Sinking  
\- More dense substances sink.

Immiscible Liquids  
\- Immiscible liquids do not mix.  
\- Less dense liquids float on top.

\==================================================  
WATER AND DENSITY  
\==================================================

Warm Water  
\- Warm water particles spread further apart.  
\- Warm water is less dense.

Cold Water  
\- Cold water particles are closer together.  
\- Cold water is more dense.

Ice  
\- Ice is less dense than liquid water.  
\- Ice floats on water.

\==================================================  
DENSITY OF AIR  
\==================================================

Air  
\- Air has mass and volume.  
\- Air has density.
