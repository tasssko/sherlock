@Y7 HISTORY — ELIZABETH I MASTER REVISION DOCUMENT

\==================================================  
ELIZABETH I (1558–1603)  
\==================================================

Who was Elizabeth I?  
\- Elizabeth I was the daughter of Henry VIII and Anne Boleyn.  
\- She became Queen of England in 1558 after the death of Mary I.  
\- Elizabeth had a difficult childhood.  
\- Her mother, Anne Boleyn, was executed when Elizabeth was young.  
\- Elizabeth was highly educated and intelligent.

\==================================================  
PROBLEMS ELIZABETH FACED  
\==================================================

Elizabeth faced four main problems when she became Queen:  
\- threat of invasion  
\- religious division  
\- succession  
\- lack of money

\--------------------------------------------------  
THREAT OF INVASION  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- King Francis II of France claimed Elizabeth was illegitimate.  
\- Mary, Queen of Scots also claimed to be the rightful Queen of England.  
\- France and Spain both threatened England.  
\- Philip II of Spain became Elizabeth’s greatest foreign threat.  
\- English sailors attacked Spanish ships carrying treasure from the Americas.  
\- Elizabeth supported Protestant rebels in the Spanish Netherlands.

\--------------------------------------------------  
RELIGIOUS DIVISION  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- England had experienced religious changes under Henry VIII, Edward VI and Mary I.  
\- Catholics and Protestants disagreed strongly about religion.  
\- Elizabeth was Protestant but wanted compromise.  
\- She tried to keep both Catholics and Protestants reasonably satisfied.

\--------------------------------------------------  
THE ISSUE OF SUCCESSION  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Elizabeth was unmarried.  
\- Many people believed women were not suitable rulers.  
\- People worried about who would inherit the throne.  
\- Elizabeth was expected to marry and produce a male heir.

\--------------------------------------------------  
THE LACK OF MONEY  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- England’s treasury had very little money when Elizabeth became Queen.  
\- England needed money to defend itself from invasion.  
\- Poor harvests had increased poverty and begging.

\==================================================  
THE ELIZABETHAN RELIGIOUS SETTLEMENT  
\==================================================

\- Elizabeth wanted a religious settlement that would unite the country.  
\- In 1559 she introduced two important laws.

\--------------------------------------------------  
THE ACT OF SUPREMACY (1559)  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- The Act of Supremacy made Elizabeth Supreme Governor of the Church of England.  
\- Elizabeth did not use the title “Supreme Head.”  
\- This wording was designed to make Catholics more accepting of the settlement.

\--------------------------------------------------  
THE ACT OF UNIFORMITY (1559)  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- The Act of Uniformity introduced a new version of the Book of Common Prayer.  
\- Some Catholic features remained in churches.  
\- People had to attend Church of England services on Sundays and holy days.  
\- Elizabeth wanted moderate religious compromise.

\==================================================  
PURITANS AND CATHOLICS  
\==================================================

Puritans  
\- Puritans were extreme Protestants.  
\- They wanted further Protestant reforms.  
\- Some Puritans in Parliament criticised Elizabeth’s settlement.

Catholics  
\- Catholics believed Elizabeth’s settlement was too Protestant.  
\- Some Catholics supported Mary, Queen of Scots as the rightful ruler.  
\- Elizabeth appointed Sir Francis Walsingham to help protect England from Catholic threats.

\==================================================  
MARY, QUEEN OF SCOTS  
\==================================================

\- Mary, Queen of Scots was the daughter of James V of Scotland.  
\- Catholics viewed Mary as a possible replacement for Elizabeth.  
\- Mary became the focus of several Catholic plots.

\==================================================  
THE NORTHERN REVOLT (1569)  
\==================================================

Causes  
\- Catholic nobles wanted Mary to replace Elizabeth.  
\- The Duke of Norfolk supported Mary.

Events  
\- The Earls of Northumberland and Westmorland led the revolt.  
\- Rebels hoped for support from Spain.  
\- Elizabeth sent an army of 12,000 soldiers.

Consequences  
\- The rebellion failed.  
\- Hundreds of rebels were executed.  
\- Northumberland was executed in 1572\.  
\- Westmorland escaped abroad.

\==================================================  
THE RIDOLFI PLOT (1571)  
\==================================================

\- Roberto di Ridolfi planned to free Mary from prison.  
\- The plot aimed to marry Mary to the Duke of Norfolk.  
\- Spain would then support the invasion of England.  
\- Sir Francis Walsingham uncovered the plot.  
\- Norfolk was executed in 1572\.

\==================================================  
THE THROCKMORTON PLOT (1583)  
\==================================================

\- Francis Throckmorton secretly communicated with the Spanish ambassador.  
\- The plot aimed to:  
  \- invade England  
  \- murder Elizabeth  
  \- place Mary on the throne  
\- Throckmorton was executed.  
\- Mary avoided execution because direct proof was lacking.

\==================================================  
THE BABINGTON PLOT (1586)  
\==================================================

\- Anthony Babington wrote letters to Mary.  
\- The letters discussed killing Elizabeth.  
\- Walsingham’s spies intercepted the letters.  
\- Mary was found guilty of treason.

\--------------------------------------------------  
EXECUTION OF MARY, QUEEN OF SCOTS  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Elizabeth reluctantly signed Mary’s death warrant.  
\- Mary was executed on 8 February 1587\.  
\- Elizabeth later claimed the execution happened against her wishes.

\==================================================  
THE SPANISH ARMADA (1588)  
\==================================================

Why Spain attacked England  
\- Philip II wanted England to return to Catholicism.  
\- English sailors attacked Spanish treasure ships.  
\- Elizabeth supported Protestant rebels against Spain.  
\- Mary, Queen of Scots had been executed.

\--------------------------------------------------  
THE SPANISH PLAN  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Spain prepared a fleet of 130 ships.  
\- The Duke of Medina Sidonia commanded the Armada.  
\- Spain planned to collect troops from the Netherlands and invade England.

\--------------------------------------------------  
WHY ENGLAND HAD ADVANTAGES  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- English ships were smaller and faster.  
\- English cannons could fire more quickly.  
\- England had a strong spy network.  
\- Storms damaged many Spanish ships.

\--------------------------------------------------  
KEY EVENTS OF THE ARMADA  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Small battles took place at Plymouth, Portland Bill and the Isle of Wight.  
\- The Spanish reached Calais.  
\- The English sent fireships into the Spanish fleet.  
\- The Spanish formation broke apart.  
\- The Battle of Gravelines caused severe Spanish losses.  
\- The Armada sailed around Scotland and Ireland to return home.

\--------------------------------------------------  
CONSEQUENCES OF THE ARMADA  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Many Spanish ships were destroyed.  
\- Thousands of Spanish sailors died.  
\- Elizabeth’s popularity increased greatly.  
\- England remained Protestant.

\==================================================  
ELIZABETH’S GOVERNMENT  
\==================================================

\--------------------------------------------------  
WILLIAM CECIL  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- William Cecil was Elizabeth’s Principal Secretary.  
\- He helped reduce government spending.  
\- Cecil opposed allowing Catholics back onto the English throne.

\--------------------------------------------------  
SIR FRANCIS WALSINGHAM  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Walsingham led Elizabeth’s intelligence service.  
\- He developed a large spy network.  
\- He uncovered Catholic plots against Elizabeth.

\--------------------------------------------------  
ROBERT DUDLEY  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Robert Dudley was Earl of Leicester.  
\- He was one of Elizabeth’s favourite courtiers.  
\- He helped Elizabeth maintain support.

\==================================================  
ELIZABETH AND PARLIAMENT  
\==================================================

\- Elizabeth called Parliament only thirteen times.  
\- Parliament approved taxes and laws.  
\- Elizabeth disliked Parliament interfering in religion and succession.  
\- Elizabeth generally maintained good relations with Parliament.

\==================================================  
THE SUCCESSION  
\==================================================

\- Elizabeth never married.  
\- She had no children.  
\- Before her death, she chose James VI of Scotland as her heir.  
\- James became James I of England.

\==================================================  
KEY TERMS  
\==================================================

Religious Settlement  
\- Elizabeth’s attempt to create religious compromise in England.

Supreme Governor  
\- Title given to Elizabeth as head of the Church of England.

Puritan  
\- An extreme Protestant who wanted more reform.

Recusant  
\- A person who refused to attend Church of England services.

Treason  
\- Crimes against the monarch or country.

Armada  
\- A large fleet of warships.

Spy network  
\- A system of spies gathering secret information.

Succession  
\- The order in which rulers inherit the throne.
