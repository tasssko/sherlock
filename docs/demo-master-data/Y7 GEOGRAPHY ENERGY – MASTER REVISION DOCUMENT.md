\==================================================  
1\. EARLY HUMAN ENERGY USE  
\==================================================

\- Early humans mainly burned wood, a type of biomass.  
\- Biomass \= energy from organic material such as:  
  \- wood  
  \- plants  
  \- food waste

\==================================================  
2\. ENERGY AND DEVELOPMENT  
\==================================================

\- Electricity access varies around the world.  
\- Some countries still have low electricity access.  
\- Access to electricity increased between 1994 and 2017\.  
\- Countries rely on different main energy sources:  
  \- coal  
  \- gas  
  \- hydroelectric power  
  \- renewable energy

\==================================================  
3\. HOW COUNTRIES USE ENERGY DIFFERENTLY  
\==================================================

China:  
\- Around 70% of energy is used in industry.  
\- China has lots of manufacturing.  
\- Only around 15% is used for transport.

USA:  
\- Around 35% of energy is used in industry.  
\- The USA has more service jobs.  
\- Around 37% is used for transport.  
\- Homes use more energy because:  
  \- houses are larger  
  \- there are more appliances  
  \- more people use cars

\==================================================  
4\. WHY GLOBAL ENERGY USE INCREASES  
\==================================================

\- More wealth leads to:  
  \- more technology  
  \- more travel  
  \- more products

\- Growing populations lead to:  
  \- more transport  
  \- more homes  
  \- more factories

\- Countries look for:  
  \- new energy sources  
  \- more efficient energy sources

\==================================================  
5\. FOSSIL FUELS  
\==================================================

Coal:  
\- Formed from dead trees and plants in swamps.

Oil and Gas:  
\- Formed from tiny sea creatures buried under sediment.

Facts:  
\- In 2024, 87% of global energy came from fossil fuels.  
\- Coal demand was 165 exajoules.  
\- 83% of coal demand came from Asia.  
\- Oil provides 33.6% of global energy.

\==================================================  
6\. PROBLEMS WITH FOSSIL FUELS  
\==================================================

\- Fossil fuels cause air pollution.  
\- Air pollution can cause:  
  \- asthma  
  \- lung cancer  
  \- breathing problems

\- Fossil fuels release greenhouse gases.  
\- Greenhouse gases cause climate change.  
\- Fossil fuels are non-renewable.  
\- Non-renewable means they will eventually run out.

\==================================================  
7\. AIR POLLUTION  
\==================================================

Causes of Air Pollution:  
\- cars  
\- factories  
\- burning fuels

Effects of Air Pollution:  
\- breathing problems  
\- dirty air  
\- environmental damage

Fact:  
\- In 2019, 14 of the world’s 20 most polluted cities were in India.

\==================================================  
8\. RENEWABLE ENERGY SOURCES  
\==================================================

Solar Energy:  
\- Sunlight is turned into electricity.  
\- Provided around 5% of UK electricity in 2023\.

Wind Energy:  
\- Wind turns turbines to make electricity.  
\- Provides around 28% of UK electricity.

Hydroelectric Power:  
\- Moving water turns turbines.  
\- Provides around 15% of global electricity.

Biomass:  
\- Organic material is burned for heat and electricity.  
\- Provides around 12% of global energy.

Tidal Energy:  
\- Uses the rise and fall of tides.  
\- France has a 240 MW tidal power station.

Geothermal Energy:  
\- Uses heat from inside the Earth.  
\- Iceland gets around 90% of its heating from geothermal energy.

\==================================================  
9\. ENERGY SECURITY  
\==================================================

Good Energy Security:  
\- A country produces enough energy for its needs.

Poor Energy Security:  
\- A country relies heavily on energy imports.

Energy-rich regions:  
\- Middle East  
\- Africa  
\- Russia

Regions that use more energy than they produce:  
\- Europe  
\- South Asia  
\- East Asia
