AIR PRESSURE  
\==================================================

What is Air Pressure?  
\- Air pressure is the weight of air pressing down on Earth.  
\- Air pressure is highest near the ground because more air is pushing down from above.

\--------------------------------------------------  
Rising and Sinking Air: Wet vs Dry Weather  
\--------------------------------------------------

Wet Weather (Low Pressure)  
\- Warm air rises.  
\- Rising air cools.  
\- Water vapour condenses into droplets to form clouds.  
\- Droplets join together and fall as rain.

Key Features of Low Pressure  
\- Cloudy weather  
\- Rain  
\- Rising air  
\- Winds spiral inwards and anticlockwise

Dry Weather (High Pressure)  
\- Cool air sinks.  
\- Sinking air warms up.  
\- Clouds disappear.  
\- Sky becomes clear.

Key Features of High Pressure  
\- Dry weather  
\- Clear skies  
\- Sinking air  
\- Winds spiral outwards and clockwise

\==================================================  
WIND AND ISOBARS  
\==================================================

Wind  
\- Wind is air moving from high pressure to low pressure.

Isobars  
\- Isobars are lines on weather maps showing air pressure.

Isobar Spacing  
\- Close isobars \= strong winds  
\- Wide isobars \= light winds

Wind Patterns

Low Pressure Systems  
\- Winds spiral inwards  
\- Winds move anticlockwise

High Pressure Systems  
\- Winds spiral outwards  
\- Winds move clockwise

\==================================================  
AIR MASSES AND TEMPERATURE  
\==================================================

What is an Air Mass?  
\- An air mass is a large body of air with similar temperature and moisture.

Types of Air Masses

Maritime  
\- Forms over oceans  
\- Moist

Continental  
\- Forms over land  
\- Dry

Polar / Arctic  
\- Forms near the North Pole  
\- Cold

Tropical  
\- Forms near the Equator  
\- Warm

Important Facts  
\- Wind moves air masses over the UK.  
\- Different air masses bring different weather conditions.  
\- Air masses can be warm and damp or cold and dry.

\==================================================  
TORNADOES  
\==================================================

How Tornadoes Form  
1\. Warm and cold air meet.  
2\. A spinning horizontal tube of air forms.  
3\. Rising warm air tilts the tube upright.  
4\. A rotating column of air develops.  
5\. A supercell thunderstorm forms.  
6\. A tornado extends downward and may reach the ground.

EF Scale  
The EF Scale measures tornadoes using:  
\- Estimated wind speed  
\- Amount of damage caused

\==================================================  
HURRICANES (CYCLONES / TYPHOONS)  
\==================================================

What are Hurricanes?  
\- Huge rotating storms formed from clusters of thunderstorms.  
\- Warm, moist rising air provides the energy.

Hurricane Facts  
\- Most active in the Atlantic from mid-August to early October.  
\- Around 6–7 hurricanes form each year.  
\- A storm becomes a hurricane at 74 mph winds.  
\- Hurricanes are measured using the Saffir-Simpson Scale (Category 1–5).

\--------------------------------------------------  
Parts of a Hurricane  
\--------------------------------------------------

Eye  
\- Calm centre  
\- Around 20–30 miles wide

Eye Wall  
\- Strongest winds

Rainbands  
\- Spiralling clouds producing heavy rain

\--------------------------------------------------  
Storm Surge  
\--------------------------------------------------

What is a Storm Surge?  
\- The most dangerous part of a hurricane.  
\- Strong winds push sea water inland.

Facts About Storm Surges  
\- Can reach up to 20 feet high  
\- Can be 100 miles wide  
\- Cause around 90% of hurricane-related deaths

\--------------------------------------------------  
Why Hurricanes Matter  
\--------------------------------------------------

\- Move heat from the equator toward the poles.  
\- Help keep Earth’s temperature balanced.

MONSOONS AND BANGLADESH FLOODING  
\==================================================

What is a Monsoon?  
\- A seasonal wind bringing very heavy rain.  
\- In South Asia, monsoons happen from June to September.

Why Bangladesh Floods Easily

1\. Much of the country is on flat floodplains.  
2\. Three large rivers meet there:  
   \- Ganges  
   \- Brahmaputra  
   \- Meghna  
3\. Heavy monsoon rain fills rivers quickly.

BHANSAN CHAR ISLAND  
\==================================================

Formation  
\- Formed in 2006 from silt building up until it rose above sea level.

\--------------------------------------------------  
Refugee Camp on Bhansan Char  
\--------------------------------------------------

Why Camps Were Built There  
\- Other camps were overcrowded.  
\- The island had space for many people.  
\- Flood defences and shelters were built.  
\- Mainly intended for Rohingya refugees.

Why the Rohingya Fled Myanmar  
\- Violence  
\- Discrimination  
\- Danger

UN Concerns About Bhansan Char  
1\. Risk of storm surges and cyclones.  
2\. Flood protection may not be strong enough during severe storms.  
3\. The island is low and made of silt, so flooding is likely.

DROUGHTS, BUSHFIRES AND WILDFIRES  
\==================================================

Drought

Definition  
\- A long period of below-average rainfall.

Effects  
\- Increases the risk of bushfires.

\--------------------------------------------------  
Bushfires  
\--------------------------------------------------

Definition  
\- Fast-spreading wildfires during drought.

Important Fact  
\- Bushfires are a secondary hazard, not a type of weather.

\--------------------------------------------------  
Wildfires  
\--------------------------------------------------

Definition  
\- Large uncontrolled fires in forests or grasslands.

Wildfire Facts  
\- Burn millions of acres each year.  
\- About 90% are caused by humans.

Human Causes Include  
\- Campfires  
\- Cigarettes  
\- Arson  
\- Machinery

\==================================================  
THE FIRE TRIANGLE  
\==================================================

Three things are needed for a wildfire:

1\. Heat  
Sources include:  
\- The sun  
\- Lightning  
\- Matches  
\- Machinery

2\. Fuel  
Examples:  
\- Dry grass  
\- Leaves  
\- Trees  
\- Flammable plants

3\. Oxygen  
\- Wind increases oxygen supply and spreads flames.

\==================================================  
CONDITIONS INCREASING WILDFIRE RISK  
\==================================================

\- Hot, dry weather  
\- Drought  
\- El Niño weather patterns

\==================================================  
EL NIÑO  
\==================================================

Definition  
\- A natural climate pattern caused by warming ocean surface temperatures in the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean.

Key Facts  
\- Occurs every 2–7 years.  
\- Creates warmer and drier conditions in some regions.

\==================================================  
ENVIRONMENTAL BENEFITS OF WILDFIRES  
\==================================================

\- Remove diseased plants.  
\- Remove harmful insects.  
\- Open forest canopies for sunlight.  
\- Help ecosystems regenerate.  
\- Help new plants grow.
